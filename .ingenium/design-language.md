# Attic Jiu Jitsu Design Language

## Brand summary and intended perception

Attic Jiu Jitsu should feel like a serious local academy with a welcoming door. The design needs to communicate three things at once: credible coaching, a strong team environment, and a low-friction path for complete beginners.

## Target audience

- Adults who want to get fitter, learn a real skill, and start BJJ without feeling intimidated
- Parents comparing kids and teens activities in Carlow
- More experienced grapplers looking for gi, no-gi, sparring, and a solid team culture

The design should signal: approachable, disciplined, local, authentic, and physically grounded.

## Visual direction

Use a high-contrast, monochrome-led system shaped by real gym photography. The site should feel cleaner than the current Wix build while preserving its gritty mat-side energy.

## Color palette

- `ink`: `#0b0b0d` for major backgrounds and hero overlays
- `graphite`: `#17171b` for cards and dark surfaces
- `bone`: `#f4efe7` for warm light backgrounds
- `fog`: `#d8d0c4` for muted borders and supporting text on dark surfaces
- `bronze`: `#b48a51` for primary accents, emphasis, and active states
- `chalk`: `#f8f5ef` for high-contrast text on dark backgrounds

## Typography

- Headings: `Barlow Condensed` in bold or semibold uppercase or title case
- Body: `Source Sans 3`
- Use the display face sparingly but decisively for hero headlines, section labels, stat blocks, and CTA emphasis
- Keep body copy readable, left-aligned, and compact

## Existing site font evaluation

- The current site uses a condensed athletic display direction that suits the category.
- Preserve that main direction rather than shifting into a generic startup style.
- Replace the inconsistent Wix support-font mix with one clean supporting sans.

## Spacing and layout

- Use wide horizontal padding with strong vertical rhythm
- Alternate dark and warm-light sections to keep pace across long pages
- Keep content in a centered max width around `1200px`
- Use layered panels and framed image blocks instead of flat full-width stacks everywhere

## Radius, shadow, and stroke treatment

- Radius: `20px` on major cards and image frames
- Borders: thin warm or translucent white strokes
- Shadows: soft, low-spread shadows only on light sections
- Prefer subtle depth over floating glossy cards

## Buttons and interaction

- Primary button: bronze fill, dark text, square-ish athletic shape, slight lift on hover
- Secondary button: transparent or dark fill with border
- Buttons should feel firm and direct, not pillowy or playful

## Cards and containers

- Program and pricing cards use dark surfaces with warm borders or light surfaces with dark headings
- Include small uppercase labels above major card titles
- Use photography blocks with gradient overlays to keep real-gym authenticity

## Navigation and footer

- Sticky dark header
- Simple primary nav with one highlighted CTA
- Footer should reinforce location, contact details, social links, and the main CTA

## Imagery and art direction

- Reuse existing real training photography from the current website
- Hero uses a full-bleed gym or training image with a dark overlay
- Gallery images should feel documentary, not over-edited
- Favor crops with coaching, hand fighting, movement, and team interaction

## Motion

- Use restrained entrance motion: fade-up and slight translate on key hero and card elements
- Hover interactions should be subtle, not flashy
- Avoid parallax, sliders, or decorative animation overload

## Accessibility and responsive rules

- Keep contrast high on every CTA and body text block
- Ensure mobile nav, cards, tables, and image grids collapse cleanly
- Maintain readable line lengths and avoid cramped uppercase paragraphs

## Page-specific notes

- Home: strongest hero treatment, quick audience paths, proof stack, and final form section
- About us: coach credibility and culture first
- Classes: make class types easy to compare without depending on the external booking UI
- Memberships: keep pricing transparent and grouped clearly
- Gallery: images should serve trust and atmosphere, not filler

## Existing site visual clues to preserve, adapt, or replace

Preserve:
- Real photography
- Coach bios and black belt credibility
- Monochrome visual base
- Transparent pricing

Adapt:
- Hero image treatment into a stronger high-contrast landing section
- Gallery assets into more curated framed layouts
- Existing academy feel into a cleaner page hierarchy

Replace:
- Wix template styling
- Inconsistent type pairings
- Hidden navigation structure and fragmented CTA language
- Flat, repetitive section stacking

## Do

- Use real photography throughout the experience
- Keep the condensed athletic headline direction
- Make beginner reassurance visible in the first screen
- Use bronze accent sparingly for emphasis and action
- Keep pages bold, clean, and conversion-oriented

## Do not

- Do not introduce purple, pastel, or glossy SaaS styling
- Do not use stock martial arts imagery
- Do not overload pages with generic animation
- Do not hide core pages behind menu overflow patterns
- Do not drift away from the monochrome-plus-accent system without updating this file first
